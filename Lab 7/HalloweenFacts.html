10 spooky halloween facts

Halloween originated with the ancient Celtic festival of Samhain, when people would light bonfires and dress up in costumes to ward off ghosts. The Celtics would celebrate their new year on November 1st. They also believed that the night of the 31st ghosts of the dead returned to earth and the line between the living and the dead became blurred. Here are some facts about the holiday.
  1. Because the movie Halloween(1978) was on such a tight budget, they have to use the cheapest mask they could find for the character Michel Myers. Which turned out to be a Star Trek William Shatner mask.

  2. The first Jack O'Lanterns were actually made from turnips.

  3. Samhainophobia is the fear of Halloween.

  4. According to Irish legend, Jack O'Lanterns are named after a stingy man named jack who, because he tricked the devil several times, was forbidden entrance into both heaven and hell. He was condemned to wander the Earth, waving his lanter to lead people away from their paths.

  5. "Souling" is a medieval Christian precursor to modern-day trick-or-treating. On Hallowmas (November 1), the poor would go door-to-door offering prayers for the dead in exchange for soul cakes.

  6. Ireland is believed to be the birthplace of Halloween.

  7. According to tradition, if a person wears his or her clothes inside out and then walks backwards on Halloween, he or she will see a witch at midnight.

  8. Dressing up as ghouls and other creatures originated from the ancient Celts. They would disguise themselves as demons and spirits as they believed that this would allow them to escape the notice of real spirits that wander the streets during the Samhain.

  9. Harry Houdini the famous magician died on October 31, 1926.

  10. The longest haunted house in the world is "Factory of Terror" in Canton, Ohio.
